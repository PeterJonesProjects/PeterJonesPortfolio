Application of Learning Analytics to 'Massive Online Open Course' Data

By Peter Jones

November 4, 2022

The project discussed here involved applying learning analytics to the data collected from multiple runs of a Massive Open Online Course (MOOC). The aim was to gain insights that could help course providers make improvements to the course content and delivery. The project followed the CRISP-DM process for data mining, which involves a cyclical approach to data analysis that includes six phases: business understanding, data understanding, data preparation, modeling, evaluation, and deployment.

The project plan involved analyzing the average scores and ranges for the final test of the course in each country to compare the performance of different groups of learners. The data used included information on the country of origin of each pupil, questions answered, and response data. However, the data quality was not perfect, and the detected countries were too diffuse to analyze. Therefore, the project team had to prepare the data and filter out outliers to make it useful for analysis.

The analysis revealed that North America was the most successful continent, followed by Asia and Europe. South America was found to be the least successful, but the inference was not very reliable due to the small data set. The project team then looked for a connection between video engagement and the outcome of the tests to determine if watching and engaging with the course content could increase success rates.

The evaluation of the data revealed a weak positive relationship, which improved when South America was removed as an outlier. However, there was not enough evidence to say that video engagement drives success. The project team suggested that a bigger data set of people who had completed the test and more information on engagement, such as time spent reading notes, would provide a better picture of engagement and enable them to draw out connections more accurately.

The project utilized major R libraries, including ggplot2, dplyr, and ProjectTemplate, to preprocess and analyze the data. The project team emphasized proper exploration and preprocessing of the data to ensure accurate insights. The project results provided insights that could help course providers make informed decisions and improve the course content and delivery.
